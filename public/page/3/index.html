Working slow and fast: How to benefit from both modes of thinking

2021-03-17 ::
Whenever I want go deep, I am distracted by things “I need to do”. And that’s true: If I do not adress them, my work will suffer. And whenever do I block out everything that is not related to the deeper task at hand, I am constantly on the lookout for fast decisions that require little understanding, but help me feel productive. In effect, that work dampens real progress. But when I do succeed at working slow and coming up with a feasible solution to a deeper problem, I manage to reach decisions on other questions much faster.
